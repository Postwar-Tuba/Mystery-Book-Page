Shadows linger where memory falters, and whispers trace the edge of thought like a knife’s shadow. When does a story truly end? Perhaps it is not the death of flesh, but the fading of what we leave behind. Secrets are the only truths that survive.

Every choice echoes, whether seen or unseen, and the paths we think we walk were drawn long before our first steps. What is destiny if not a mirror of our own fears? Questions remain even when answers vanish.

Names carved into stone, letters written in candle smoke. Identity slips like sand through fingers, and we cling to illusions of permanence. Conflict feeds itself; boredom, the silent predator of human ambition.

Every dawn washes away yesterday, every shadow conceals the truth. From echoes of footsteps to the roar of machines, from ink to blood, nothing remains untouched by time. And yet, we persist.

What becomes of wonder when it is expected? When awe turns routine, the extraordinary dissolves into the mundane. Pride and anonymity, the masks we wear. Some rule like shepherds, some run like prey. You witness events beyond comprehension, and still the clock ticks. Just another moment.

And in the quiet, everything waits. You awaken once again.
